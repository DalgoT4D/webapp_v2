# Motion

Dalgo uses **`tw-animate-css`** for keyframe animations and Tailwind's
`transition-*` utilities for state changes. Keep motion subtle and fast — the app
is data-dense; animation should aid, not distract.

## Transitions

Default interaction transition is `transition-all` (used on buttons, cards).
Duration ~150–200ms. Hover a button (UI/Button) to see it.

<div style={{ display: 'flex', gap: 16, marginTop: 12 }}>
  {['transition-all', 'transition-colors', 'transition-opacity'].map((cls) => (
    <code key={cls} style={{ fontSize: 12 }}>{cls}</code>
  ))}
</div>

## Spinner

The standard loading indicator is `Loader2` + `animate-spin` (used in 44 files).

<div style={{ display: 'flex', alignItems: 'center', gap: 12, marginTop: 12 }}>
  <Loader2 className="animate-spin" style={{ width: 24, height: 24, color: 'var(--primary)' }} />
  <code style={{ fontSize: 12 }}>{'<Loader2 className="animate-spin" />'}</code>
</div>

## Enter/exit (Radix overlays)

Dialogs, popovers, tooltips use `tw-animate-css` data-state animations:
`fade-in-0`, `zoom-in-95`, `slide-in-from-*`. See `ui/tooltip.tsx` and
`ui/dialog.tsx` for the canonical recipe — don't roll your own.

<div style={{ marginTop: 12 }}>
  <code style={{ fontSize: 11 }}>
    animate-in fade-in-0 zoom-in-95 data-[state=closed]:animate-out
  </code>
</div>
